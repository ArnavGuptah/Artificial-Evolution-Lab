# Experiment 2 — Long-Term Ecosystem Stability and Species Dominance

## Runtime
16 minutes

## Parameter Changes
None (same parameters as Experiment 1)

---

# Major Observations

## 1. Persistent Green Species Survival

Green organisms remained present throughout nearly the entire simulation lifespan.

Observed behaviors:
- Green population fluctuated frequently.
- Population size sometimes reduced significantly.
- Despite fluctuations, green organisms never completely vanished.
- Other color groups periodically disappeared entirely.

### Interpretation
This suggests that green organisms may represent a stable and energy-efficient evolutionary strategy capable of surviving long-term environmental pressure.

Possible characteristics:
- moderate movement speed
- efficient metabolism
- balanced reproduction-energy tradeoff

---

# 2. Cyclic Dominance Shifts

Species dominance changed repeatedly during the simulation.

Observed sequence examples:
- Green dominance
- Yellow emergence
- Red-yellow dominance
- Green recovery
- Blue late-stage dominance

These shifts occurred multiple times throughout the runtime.

### Interpretation
The ecosystem appears to exhibit emergent dominance oscillations instead of permanent monopolization by one species.

This resembles:
- ecological succession
- adaptive competition cycles
- boom-bust population dynamics

---

# 3. Population Collapse and Recovery

Large populations occasionally vanished rapidly.

Observed pattern:
- population growth
- overcrowding
- sharp decline
- emergence of new dominant organisms

### Interpretation
This suggests instability caused by:
- food competition
- movement energy costs
- reproductive pressure

The ecosystem may naturally cycle between expansion and collapse phases.

---

# 4. Territorial and Colony Formation

Blue organisms frequently remained clustered in localized regions.

Observed behaviors:
- blue organisms formed colonies
- clusters stayed spatially grouped
- descendants remained near parent organisms
- territorial regions persisted over time

Green organisms also displayed territorial clustering in later stages.

### Interpretation
This behavior was not explicitly programmed and likely emerged from:
- localized reproduction
- food proximity
- movement limitations

This indicates emergent territorial organization.

---

# 5. Food Abundance Effects

During periods of abundant food:
- larger organisms appeared more frequently
- movement activity increased
- territorial structures weakened temporarily
- later recolonization occurred

Observed progression:
small green organisms → large green organisms

Later:
- light blue organisms emerged
- dark blue variants appeared afterward

### Interpretation
Food abundance appears to strongly influence:
- organism size
- expansion behavior
- species diversification
- territorial pressure

---

# 6. Emergence of Long-Term Blue Lineages

After extended runtime:
- light blue organisms emerged
- dark blue organisms later appeared
- blue populations increased gradually
- blue dominance occurred during late stages

Observed behavior:
- blue organisms strongly preferred clustered colony existence

### Interpretation
This may indicate evolution toward:
- territorial specialization
- low-risk local survival
- colony-based persistence strategies

---

# 7. Stable Green Baseline Population

One of the most consistent observations:

Even when:
- yellow species disappeared
- red populations collapsed
- blue dominance changed

green organisms remained present.

### Interpretation
Green organisms may represent a foundational or equilibrium species within the ecosystem.

This suggests:
- adaptive resilience
- ecological stability
- sustainable energy strategy

---

# Key Emergent Behaviors Observed

- territorial clustering
- colony persistence
- cyclic species dominance
- long-term stable species survival
- population oscillation
- spatial lineage grouping
- adaptive resource competition

None of these behaviors were directly hardcoded.

---

# Conclusion

Experiment 2 strongly suggests that the ecosystem is exhibiting genuine emergent evolutionary dynamics rather than random particle movement.

Repeated observations across long runtime indicate:
- stable ecological behaviors
- adaptive specialization
- territorial emergence
- resource-driven evolutionary pressure

Green organisms demonstrated the highest long-term survivability, while blue organisms displayed increasingly territorial colony behavior during later stages of evolution.

## Quantitative Results

### Population Dynamics

- Population remained relatively stable for most of the experiment.
- Long-term population generally remained between 75 and 90 organisms.
- No major extinction event was observed.

### Speed Evolution

Observed average speed:

~1.94 → ~0.88

This represents one of the largest evolutionary shifts observed so far.

---

## Emergent Behaviors

### Evolution Toward Slow Movement

Organisms consistently evolved toward lower movement speeds.

Possible reasons:

- Lower energy expenditure
- Improved long-term survival
- Increased reproductive opportunities

This behavior emerged naturally without explicit programming.

---

### Persistent Clustering

Organisms repeatedly formed colonies around favorable locations.

No colony behavior was explicitly programmed.

---

## Interpretation

Experiment 2 provided the first strong evidence that the ecosystem naturally selects for energy-efficient organisms.

Instead of favoring speed, evolution consistently moved toward slower movement strategies.

---

## Retrospective Notes

Later experiments confirmed that speed reduction was not unique to this run.

The trend appears repeatedly across multiple environmental conditions.

Experiment 2 therefore represents the first observation of a recurring evolutionary pattern.
