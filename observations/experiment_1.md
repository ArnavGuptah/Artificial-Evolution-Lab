# Experiment 1 — Emergent Territorial Dynamics

## Runtime
15 minutes

## Observed Behaviors

### 1. Edge Survival Specialization
Slow-moving green organisms frequently survived near peripheral regions of the world. Although they often failed to compete for nearby food against fast-moving clusters, their lower movement cost appeared to support long-term survival.

### 2. Territorial Cluster Formation
Dense organism clusters formed around food-rich regions and often became semi-stationary. This territorial behavior was not explicitly programmed and emerged naturally through local competition and reproduction.

### 3. Dominance Oscillation
Population dominance shifted cyclically between yellow, red, and green organism groups over time. No single species maintained permanent dominance during early stages.

### 4. Competitive Exclusion
Fast-moving clusters frequently captured food resources before isolated nearby organisms could reach them, suggesting strong mobility advantage under competitive pressure.

### 5. Stable Green Dominance
In later stages, green organisms maintained dominance for significantly longer durations, possibly indicating discovery of a stable evolutionary strategy.
