# Emergent Behaviors

## Territorial Clustering
First observed: Experiment 1

Observed again:
- Experiment 2
- Experiment 3
- Experiment 4
- Experiment 5

Evidence:
- Organisms repeatedly formed localized colonies.
- Clusters persisted without explicit territorial code.
- Central and food-rich regions became occupied by long-lived groups.

---

## Boom-Bust Population Cycles
First observed: Experiment 3

Observed again:
- Experiment 4 (weaker form)

Evidence:
- Species expanded rapidly.
- Large clusters fragmented.
- Population declines followed local success.

---

## Edge Specialists
First observed: Early ecosystem runs

Evidence:
- Edge populations survived using lower-speed strategies.
- Different survival patterns emerged at world boundaries.

---

## Resource-Based Niche Formation
Observed in:
- Experiment 2
- Experiment 3
- Experiment 4
- Experiment 5

Evidence:
- Different lineages occupied different regions.
- Central colonies and peripheral populations behaved differently.

---

## Evolution Toward Lower Speed
Observed in all telemetry datasets.

Evidence:
- Average speed consistently trended downward over time.
- Lower energy expenditure repeatedly became a successful strategy.
